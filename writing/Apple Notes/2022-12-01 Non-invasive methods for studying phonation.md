Non-invasive methods for studying phonation

Note that you get a clean EGG signal only when 
you are phonating. Why is that? 

EGG measures the conductivity of the glottis, so a clean signal is only possible when the glottis is closed, and at its most conductive.

Move your larynx up and down a bit while speaking or voicing. Does anything change?

Yes: amplitude changes when the larynx moves, and we observed clipping of the signal.

Note what happens in very soft voice with Normalize: On: the normalized EGG becomes like a very noisy sine wave. Why does this happen?

Speaking with a soft voice can result in EGG not producing a clear signal, as the movements of the glottis are harder to detect.
