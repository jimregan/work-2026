This paper presents a system for text to speech for isolated Estonian words, concentrating on a pair of phonological challenges that are unmarked in the orthography. The main contributions of the paper are the description of the system, and its evaluation. The paper is well written and easy to follow.

The introduction could benefit from a short description of phonological quantity as it pertains to Estonian. (Another paper under review for NoDaLiDa simply adds “(length)”, although this is in reference to Icelandic.)

The first paragraph on page 2 speaks about the use of Vabamorf for the morphological parsing of Estonian. The level of detail is somewhat confusing, particularly as section 2.2 makes it quite clear that this parsing is not employed in this work.

Although the authors motivate the use of Merlin as a base on which to build their system, this choice is nonetheless somewhat unusual, given that Merlin has not been updated in five years, and the framework on which it is built (Theano) is no longer maintained. For future work the authors may wish to compare with more recent systems, such as Matcha-TTS (https://arxiv.org/abs/2309.03199) which offers similar benefits to Merlin in terms of data requirements and control, while also offering less robotic synthesis.
