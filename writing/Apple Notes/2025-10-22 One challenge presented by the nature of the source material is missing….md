One challenge presented by the nature of the source material is missing, or partial, use of diacritics in foreign names: Czech names, for example, are typically written without carons, but are still expected to be pronounced correctly, which Braxen has handled quite well, but the additional ambiguity of the characters is absent in the donor language.

Another issue is accidental miscategorisation of words: ‘Gražinytė’ is marked as ‘lat’ (Latin), rather than ‘lit’ (Lithuanian).
