Navigating afterworks for the possibly-on-the-spectrum person.

If you’re definitely neurotypical, this is not for you. It won’t even make sense to you. This is where you should stop reading.

The first thing to be aware of is that there is a possibly unclear division between:
1. being welcome to join
2. being personally invited, beyond a general group invitation
3. receiving an offer to have drinks purchased for you

Point 1: everyone is welcome, no exceptions.

Point 2: this gets a little more complicated, but here are some things to bear in mind:
	1. if it looks like you’ve gone home, it might be assumed that you have
	2. Sometimes these things are not particularly organised, and are the result of a spur-of-the-moment suggestion, so no formal invitation will have been sent. Feel free to ask: everyone is welcome
	3. If in doubt, ask! Everyone is welcome

Point 3: this is more of a personal matter.

One thing to bear in mind is that while people will offer to get drinks for you, they do expect to be repaid. Swish is the usual method.

It might seem unclear where there are reciprocal relationships, where there is an established pattern of buying each other drinks, in which case one may say to the other that there is no need to pay.
If you are not part of such a reciprocal relationship, this does not apply to you. As in any reciprocal relationship, there needs to be mutual agreement.

Generally, when someone picks up drinks for you, what are considered the typical niceties, or basic politeness, are:
1. Thanking the person for getting these items for you
2. Repaying them, or, at the very least, offering to
3. Not forming requests as orders: instead of, for example, “put it in the fridge”, consider putting the request in the form of a question: “would you mind putting it in the fridge?”

Social currency goes a long way: if you typically follow steps 1 and 2 above, you are less likely to be perceived as rude if you forget to follow step 3, for example.

If you have fallen victim to the pitfalls of being perceived as rude, don’t worry: if you’ve forgotten to thank people in the past, they’ll quickly forget about it if you remember to do so in an ongoing manner.

If, in particular, you’ve been perceived as not wanting to pay for your own drinks, you can rectify this by asking about Swish/how to repay/if this is in repayment of some other debt (that may have been forgotten about! We’re all human), ask in advance:
- “Hey, what’s your swish?”
- “I don’t have swish, how do I repay you?”
- “Just checking - are you paying me back for \[X\], or do I need to swish you?”

None of these are (typically) considered rude questions, so don’t worry about asking them.

Further to point 3: even if you’ve managed to wear out your welcome with others who would have grabbed drinks for you, you’re welcome to join on the trip to buy your own: as with afterworks, everyone is welcome, no exceptions.
