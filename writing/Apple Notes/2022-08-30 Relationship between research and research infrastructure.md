# Relationship between research and research infrastructure

Dissemination and outreach
Prototyping

How can research infrastructure suit the researcher?
The needs of a researcher are often short term, while infrastructure should be longer lasting
Everything starts with the data: collect more than the individual researcher needs, with a view towards serving the field more broadly

Trade-off: make data available in return for access to tools

There are researchers with needs for infrastructure, but unaware of the need
(Non-technical users, etc.; linguists know about the language bank, but might not have the technical knowledge required to make their data available).

SB Text tools now allow users to upload their own data, run tools, and download; there’s a possibility of publishing the data, but needs legal vetting.

Digital humanities have a crowd sourcing tool (folket ?), making a pipeline into Korp/Mink would be desirable.

Workshops within SB/SWE-CLARIN do draw non-technical users and are useful for outreach. Researchers are asked to bring their research questions, so the exercises are more focused, using their own data.

Having as many research questions as possible helps in the design process of the infrastructure.

Collaborative projects and an over-arching project can help to sustain the infrastructure, but smaller projects are more indicative of the typical needs of researchers.

KB has good and bad examples: with newspapers, they forgot that access for researchers was possible, now the system needs to be rebuilt to enable researchers to use them.
Collaborating in projects has been the best way to get a broader perspective on the needs of researchers.
KB doesn’t ask about the research question, just if the infrastructure can support the task.

SB Text infrastructure is used in quite a lot of places, but there are still too many groups who don’t realise what language technology could provide them.

Language bank infrastructure is used throughout the Nordic countries, and also being used for African languages.

Data citations needs to be fixed, because Korp is often cited instead of the corpus, or as a footnote, so the use is not counted.

(Literature search protocols in psychology could be a good basis for publishing corpus searches for more specific citations.)

SB Text should provide a set of recommendations on how to cite individual datasets/collections.

Reproducing searches, provenance in the case of updated corpora (Wikipedia, etc.) are challenges that are crucial for science.

Restricted corpora: access is controlled by the owner, not SB Text; access is via email, federated login is being worked on.

There will be a possibility to have two versions of a collection: one public, through Korp (sentence level), the other through Strix (document level).
