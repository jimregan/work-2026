‘Och’ is kind of a special case because the variants are very much specific to that word. The rules are a simplification of phonetic rules, based on the yaml that MFA claims to implement, but doesn’t: there’s before, after, match, output (I don’t remember the actual key names): so for a rule like “n > m / _ [+labial]” before is empty, match is “n”, output is “m” and after will be a regular expression: /vfpb/ or something similar.
