## **6.3. Analysis of Semantic Collapse**
The complete failure of the \texttt{sem384} model—collapsing to near-uniform cosine similarities—indicates that semantic distillation via a linear head is ill-posed when applied to a frozen SSL encoder without auxiliary supervision. We hypothesize that this is a result of **gradient incoherence**.
In the WavLM latent space, the variance is dominated by acoustic and speaker identity. The distillation objective $L_{cos} = 1 - \cos(\mathbf{e}_{speech}, \mathbf{e}_{teacher})$ attempts to map disparate speaker-dependent regions to a single speaker-agnostic semantic point. Without a speaker-aware objective to "account for" or "anchor" the speaker-specific variance, the gradients for a given sentence $x$ across speakers $S_1, S_2, ... S_n$ do not align. The resulting update for the projection head is the mean of these divergent vectors, which, in a high-dimensional space, tends toward the center of the distribution. After L2-normalization, this manifests as a collapsed output where every utterance maps to a nearly identical unit vector.
The failure of the Gradient Reversal Layer (\texttt{resem-grl}) is particularly telling. By actively stripping speaker information, the GRL removes the structural "scaffolding" that allows the model to differentiate between utterances. This confirms that **retaining** speaker information in a structured way (via the auxiliary task) is paradoxically what allows the semantic head to find its own stable manifold.
