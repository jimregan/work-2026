# Background Notes: Aidiachtaí Sealbhacha (Possessive Adjectives)

*Source: Caibidil 8 – **An Forainmneach**, leathanaigh 133–134.*

These notes summarise background grammatical rules and examples for Irish possessive adjectives, structured for later operational or agent-oriented use.

---

## 1. Aidiachtaí Sealbhacha – Foirmeacha Treise (8.3.1)

### Overview

* The tables describe **emphatic (treise)** forms of possessive adjectives **when followed by a noun**.
* Emphasis is marked using suffixes such as:

  * **‑sa / ‑se**
  * **‑san / ‑sean**
  * **‑na / ‑ne**
* Hyphens are used between:

  * two *s* sounds: `mo phas-sa`
  * two *n* sounds: `ár n‑arán‑na`
* **Non‑emphatic forms** omit these suffixes.

### Persons and Number

#### First Person

* Singular: `mo chapallsa`, `m’athairse`
* Plural: `ár mbádna`, `ár n‑earraíne`

#### Second Person

* Singular: `do chapallsa`, `d’athairse`
* Plural: `bhur mbádsa`, `bhur n‑earraíse`

#### Third Person (Masculine)

* Singular: `a chapallsan`, `a athairsean`
* Plural: `a mbádsan`, `a n‑earraísean`

#### Third Person (Feminine)

* Singular only: `a capallsa`, `a hathairse`

### Example Sentences

* *An bhfaca tú mo mhálasa?*
* *Cad a tharla dár mbronntanaisne?*
* *Chaill siad a gcluichesean.*

## 2. Emphasis Using Personal Pronouns (8.3.2)

Emphasis may also be expressed by pairing the noun phrase with an **emphatic personal pronoun**:

* `a gheansaí seisean`
* `a cóta sise`
* `a dteach siadsan`

### Examples

* *Ní raibh mórán i gceist lena mhodúil seisean.*
* *An bhfaca tú a carr sise?*
* *Tá neart le déanamh ar a bhfeirm siadsan.*

---

## 3. Aidiacht Shealbhach + dhá (8.3.3)

### Rule

When the numeral **dhá** intervenes between a possessive adjective and a noun:

* **Initial mutations apply to the noun, not to dhá itself**.

### Patterns

#### Singular (i ndiaidh dhá)

* `mo dhá charr`
* `do dhá úll`
* `a dhá fheirm`

#### Plural (i ndiaidh dhá)

* `ár dhá gcarr`
* `bhur dhá n‑úll`
* `a dhá bhfeirm`

### Examples

* *Buail do dhá bhos ar a chéile.*
* *Thit sí ar a dhá glúin.* (bain.)
* *Ar dhíol siad a dhá n‑árasán?* (iol.)

---

## 4. Forainmneach Sealbhach: **ceann** agus **cuid** (8.3.4)

Possessive pronoun function can be expressed in two main ways:

### (a) Aidiacht + **ceann** or **cuid** (with emphatic suffix)

* `mo cheannsa`
* `a gcuidsean`

### (b) Aidiacht + **cuid** + noun

* `ár gcuidne airgid`
* `bhur gcuidse iarrachtaí`

### Forms Summary

#### First Person

* Singular: `mo cheannsa`, `mo chuidse`
* Plural: `ár gceann‑na`, `ár gcuidne`

#### Second Person

* Singular: `do cheannsa`, `do chuidse`
* Plural: `bhur gceannsa`, `bhur gcuidse`

#### Third Person (Masc.)

* Singular: `a cheannsan`, `a chuidsean`
* Plural: `a gceannsan`, `a gcuidsean`

#### Third Person (Fem.)

* Singular: `a ceannsa`, `a cuidse`
