# Peer review 1

“Einstein’s theory was based on the idea that the laws of physics are the same for all observers, regardless of their relative motion. This idea was revolutionary because it challenged the classical Newtonian view of the universe, which assumed that space and time were absolute and that the laws of physics were the same for all observers regardless of their relative motion \[2\].”

You’ve written almost exactly the same words (“the laws of physics are the same for all observers, regardless of their relative motion”) both as the basis of Einstein’s theory and the Newtonian view that it replaced. It seems from the rest of the text that a qualifier in terms of inertial frames of reference was likely intended.

Aside from what seems a minor oversight, the article is clearly written, and convincing.
