Developing methodology for the creation of speech recognition systems, with a particular aim of the recognition of Swedish speech, taking into account the requirements of a number of end-user use cases, ranging from the more typical broad transcription of text, to a more focused and specific transcription, bearing in mind the needs of various speech science goals: for example, diachronic phonological research.
To this end, evaluation methodologies will need to be developed that are specifically and deliberately designed to be tailored to the speech science goals, and the speech recognition systems subsequently evaluated within these methodologies, to measure any potential improvements in a way more meaningful to the speech scientist.
Specific applications of speech recognition with regard to speech science will serve as a driver of the research efforts: the requirements of particular applications, as they become clear, will impose as yet unknown requirements on the speech recognition systems they utilise.
Much of the data available for use in the thesis, made available through various projects, imposes various challenges because of the large quantities of available data. In addition to scaling up to meet these challenges, we also wish to scale down, to more efficiently make use of smaller subsets of the data; it is hoped that methods that more efficiently make use of smaller amounts of data can be applied to other languages; with particular reference to Språkbanken Tal, the less resourced minority languages of Sweden come within its remit.



The main thrust of the efforts over the next 12 month period revolve around the development of automatic methods for extracting various speech phenomena from the output of speech recognition systems that can then be repurposed into training data for a speech recognition system that specifically incorporates these phenomena. In particular, false starts, filled pauses, and alternative pronunciations are of general interest to speech science and of particular interest to the SweTerror project.
