This paper describes a dataset of time-aligned phonetic transcriptions focusing on vowel length for Icelandic, making use of publicly available data. The authors compare the outputs of Montreal Forced Aligner, a commonly used tool for obtaining phonetic alignments, with a gold standard corpus.

The paper is well written and easy to read. CAPT is all too often based on read sentences from a single speaker, or a limited number of speakers: the authors’ empirical approach to the task of creating data for CAPT is to be commended, particularly considering the extra difficulty this introduces, and their method should serve as a road map for others wishing to produce CAPT data for other languages.

One small editorial nitpick:
In section 1 and subsection 2.3, “computer assisted language learning” is mistakenly assigned the acronym CAPT, rather than the correct CALL.
