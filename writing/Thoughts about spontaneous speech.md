**5.5.** **Some final thoughts about spontaneous**

**speech**

Spontaneous speech is different from read speech, perhaps

especially in terms of prosody (Howell and Kadi-Hanifi 1991,

Mixdorff and Pfitzinger 2005, Nakamura et al. 2008). For

instance, for read speech, phrase breaks tend to be closely

associated with units of syntax. In spoken, spontaneous utter-

ances, speakers have a lot of ‘after thoughts’ and pauses as they

think and plan the utterance. This tends to break up the formal

syntax. The topic of chunks and boundaries is an area of speech

acts, and provides an interesting milieu to explore how well

phrasal stress patterns correlate with jaw displacement patterns.

Of course, for this type of investigation, a software-implemented

algorithm is needed for factoring out vowel height differences,

as well as easily accessible ways to measure jaw displacement.
