No, it’s not the main validation claim.

What it is, is an example use case of the corpus. This sound change is, perhaps, somewhat older than even our corpus can cover among the wealthy class — and this sociological aspect is quite well known. But the emergence of this sound change more generally as a feature of Stockholm Swedish not limited to class is something that is typically dated to the mid-1990s; as it happens, we have found examples of one particular speaker who uses both forms of /i:/ in free variation; this alone would be sufficient evidence for sociolinguistics, but we want to use a well-known sound change as a proof of concept for the corpus; this is quite well studied, so it doesn’t step on the toes of an phonologists or phoneticians, but is intended to demonstrate the utility of the corpus and the measures we employ in constructing it.
