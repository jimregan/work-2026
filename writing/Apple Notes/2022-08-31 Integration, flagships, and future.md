# Integration, flagships, and future

Integration between modalities: speech & text, etc.?

Integration: between the subunits of SB, making new applications.

Folke: https://www.isof.se/arkiv-och-insamling/digitala-arkivtjanster/folke

(It would really be helpful if we had links to each other’s tools, instead of having to google)

Further developments of Folke are being worked on.

Because of restrictions on the material, there will be open data, and private data available only to researchers.

Folke and Korp have some functional overlap — KWIC, etc.: having integration between them would give, e.g., Folke better access to linguistic analysis tools.

TillTal (https://www.kth.se/is/tmh/speech-communication/tilltal-making-spoken-cultural-heritage-accessible-for-research-1.694166) project: spoken folklore pilot project that can integrate all three branches. “I want to hear when they use the ‘y’ vowel”, or “I want stories that are semantically related to horses”; this is folklore data, but the speech analysis comes from SB Tal, with text analysis via SB Text.

Doctors have an interest in having automatic dictation for their recorded patient notes; this is a highly problematic area in terms of access to data, however.

Future: what’s missing?

Corpora for vision and language
Dialogue corpora

Folklore collection has data where a dialectal word is accompanied by a drawing of the concept.

Machine translation might work for caption generation from English, but automatic caption generation has broad potential for accessibility.
The way people refer to items in a picture varies greatly between language; having visually grounded text would enable research on determining exactly how different.
Work from KTH (Bram Willemsen) on visually grounded dialogue could be relevant, but is in English only.

User-friendliness is important for downstream adoption; users may not be aware of how linguistic tools can benefit them.

Folks has not been advertised much; people can register to find out when their transcription has been approved, which encourages engagement. ISOF’s initial intention was to start small; some outreach has been made to heritage groups, but the users are coming from somewhere that can’t be pinned down.

Introducing tools like Korp to school students could increase awareness going forward; perhaps with the Linguistic Olympiad?

SB Text has had outreach events/workshops where the participants bring their own data & research questions, so the activities are more personal and practical.
User group-style activities can be helpful, where users answer each other’s questions.
