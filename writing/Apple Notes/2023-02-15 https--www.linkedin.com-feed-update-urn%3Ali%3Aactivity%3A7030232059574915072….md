Plato (Socrates, Ammon...) on large language models?

The piece of writing below is attributed to Plato. It is found in one of his dialogues, Phaedros, so the writing is really supoosed to be an account of Socrates contribution in a dialogue. And Socrates, in turn, is retelling a story about what Ammon, in Egypt, told Teuth when the latter proudly showed him his invention: writing. A complicated provenance, to say the least, but the text should be around 2400 years old and the story presumably older.

With that in mind, I think the very astute way in which this ancient text describes what large LMs can and cannot do is almost as remarkable as the fact that what large LMs can do. The background: Theuth has invented writing, and told Ammon that it will improve the wisdom and memory of Egyptians.

'\[...\] Theuth, my paragon of inventors,' replied the king, 'the discoverer of an art is not the best judge of the good or harm which will accrue to those who practise it. So it is in this case; you who are the father of writing, have out of fondness for your offspring attributed to it quite the opposite of its real function. Those who acquire it will cease to exercise their memory and become forgetful; they will rely on writing to bring things to remembrance by external signs instead of on their own internal resources. What you have discovered is a receipt for recollection, not for memory. And as for wisdom, your pupils will have the reputation for it without the reality: they will receive a quantity of information without proper instruction, and in consequence be thought very knowledgeable when they are for the most part quite ignorant. And because they are filled with the conceit of wisdom instead of real wisdom they will be a burden to society.'

Writing, no doubt, is an invention that revolutionised society, As have many tools, as will large LMs. They are tools though. Used as such, they will help, and help a lot. But I would wager my money on Plato when it comes to LMs being "intelligent", enriching "intelligence", or for that matter having much to do with whatever "artificial intelligence" is supposed to mean currently. For that, a lived experience is a necessary (but not sufficient) condition that LMs simply do not meet.
